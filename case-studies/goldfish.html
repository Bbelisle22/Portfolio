Campaign Strategy · Goldfish

Rebranding a kids' snack for adults — for one week only.

Goldfish faces a classic brand ceiling: loved by kids, written off by adults the moment they age out of the snack aisle. In October 2024, Goldfish tested a name change — "Chilean Sea Bass" — to see if humor and exclusivity could pull adults back.

Segmentation & Targeting 5Cs Analysis Campaign Evaluation Positioning

Challenge

Goldfish is universally loved by kids but largely written off by adults. The brand needed a way to re-enter the adult snacking conversation without abandoning its core, family-friendly identity.

Approach

We evaluated the campaign through a full segmentation, targeting, and positioning lens, plus a 5Cs analysis of why "targeting adults" made sense for Goldfish specifically — given its capabilities, customer behavior, and a crowded competitive set (Pringles, Lay's, Ritz).

Key insight

The DTC-only distribution choice was the smartest part of the play, and easy to overlook: selling exclusively through ChileanSeaBassCrackers.com avoided channel conflict with grocery partners while gaining direct customer data and pricing flexibility.

7 days
Length of the campaign — sold online only, with new drops daily
Sold out
Each daily drop sold out within minutes, generating real viral reach

Takeaway

It worked as attention, not yet as repositioning. The campaign generated real viral reach and a measurable, if temporary, adult-skewing perception shift — but a one-week stunt doesn't build durable adult loyalty on its own, and some public reaction (comparisons to "New Coke") flagged real brand-dilution risk if overused. Good repositioning campaigns create permission to come back; this one mostly created a moment.
