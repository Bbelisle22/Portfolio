Brand Research · Starbucks

Has the "third place" survived mobile ordering?

Starbucks built its brand promise on the "third place" — a community space between home and work. We tested whether that intended positioning still matches the actual customer experience.

Brand Equity Research Design Sentiment Analysis Gap Analysis

Challenge

Mobile ordering, drive-thru growth, and pandemic-era habits have shifted how people actually use Starbucks. We set out to test whether the intended "third place" positioning still matches reality.

Approach

We combined primary research — a 26-respondent survey of university students on brand perception, loyalty, and spend — with secondary research: AI-assisted sentiment analysis across Yelp, Google reviews, and social commentary, building an evidence-based gap analysis.

Key insight

The loyalty engine works — but the emotional "third place" promise is eroding faster than the marketing admits. 38% of negative sentiment centered on customer service and staff disengagement, directly undercutting a brand built on human connection.

44%

38.5%

Takeaway

Starbucks' digital ecosystem is genuinely excelling — a 78 ACSI score on its loyalty program, 71% weekly app-driven visits. But the gap between intended and actual brand experience is exactly the kind of signal a brand audit exists to surface before it shows up in the financials.
