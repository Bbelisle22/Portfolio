Customer Analytics · Nike

How a $51B sportswear brand turns a foot scan into a strategy.

Nike collects data at nearly every consumer touchpoint. We examined whether that infrastructure actually translates into the personalization customers notice — and whether it shows up in the business results.

Customer Analytics Consumer Insights Personalization Strategy Predictive Modeling

Challenge

Nike gathers data through apps, wearables, RFID, and social sentiment — but collecting data and using it well are different problems. We set out to understand how Nike's data infrastructure actually translates into the personalized experiences customers notice: better sizing, smarter recommendations, the right product at the right time.

Approach

We mapped Nike's full data pipeline — from collection (Nike Fit App, POS systems, wearable integrations) through processing (cloud infrastructure, customer data platforms, ML models) to activation (recommendation engines, dynamic pricing, retail personalization). Then we traced two acquisitions, Zodiac and Celect, to see whether the technology investment actually moved the business.

Key insight

Personalization isn't a customer-experience nice-to-have at Nike — it's a measurable demand-generation engine. Direct sales grew 12% and digital sales grew 32% in the year following the Zodiac and Celect integrations.

Takeaway

The Nike Fit App is the clearest example of analytics done right: it solves a real customer pain point — shoe sizing — while generating exactly the data Nike needs for inventory and design decisions. The best customer analytics investments do double duty like this: service and signal at once.
