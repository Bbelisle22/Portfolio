Growth Strategy · Rent the Runway

A fashion rental company down 98% since IPO — where's the next lever?

Rent the Runway posted a $69.9M net loss in FY24 with subscriber growth nearly flat. The question: where does a financially strained subscription business find its next growth lever, without making things worse?

Growth Strategy Financial Modeling Market Sizing Risk Mitigation

Challenge

RTR's stock has lost nearly 98% of its value since its 2021 IPO. Despite 3% revenue growth, the company posted a $69.9M net loss in FY24, and active subscriber growth has nearly flatlined at 0.5% year over year.

Approach

We started from RTR's actual core competencies — reverse logistics, inventory rotation systems, a loyal subscriber base, sustainability credibility — rather than chasing a trendy adjacent category. That discipline led us to home décor rental: a fragmented, high-growth market where RTR's existing infrastructure transfers almost directly.

Recommendation

Launch "RTR Living" as a sub-brand offering pre-styled décor bundles and à la carte rentals, piloted in NYC and LA before a full rollout — leveraging RTR's existing warehouse, shipping, and subscriber infrastructure.

Takeaway

The real strategic logic isn't "RTR should sell more stuff" — it's that rental economics favor RTR over ownership in ways that compound: higher customer lifetime value from repeat use, shared logistics lowering marginal cost, and lower acquisition cost from cross-selling an already-loyal base. We also flagged the real risks honestly — brand dilution, operational complexity — and built mitigation directly into the launch plan: sub-branding, phased rollout, third-party fulfillment for non-core functions.
